Does you 3DS's SD slot give you problems? Say no more

I used to have your same problem. I searched online for no result, asked on Discord and received no convincing reply. But having opened two 3DSs several times, I found a solution by myself.

This guide will also fix the lack of clicks and an SD card that doesn't stay in place.

It is probably the slot's broken plastics, as you can see in the two photos below. Here's how you can fix it

You'll need:

  • Small x screwdriver (mine says PH00)
  • Replacement SD slot (alix). Be careful when buyinh it that it's L shaped.
  • Something small to pry open the battery compartment, like a tweezer or a file
  • Possibly, some extra screws (ali)

Be careful to always apply some pressure

Unscrew the four screws from the back and swing the 3DS a bit to get the plate off. Note that these screws won't come off as they're held in place by some clip-in washers.

Leave the plate on a flat surface to later lay your screws on.

Remove these screws

Apply some pressure

Remove the battery from the top with something firm but not too pointy

Nice

Now read the next step and don't open the 3DS too much or the connectors might break

Use something to pry these two connectors from the bottom.

Don't open this compartment too much. The connector on the left is quite shorter than the right one.

This step is the most frightnening one. You might want to apply some extra pressure or find the right spot for the best leverage. Don't be scared. Go on.

When reassembling, check that the cables don't get stuck. It shouldn't happen but it's a possibility.

The replacement doesn't cost a lot but it's painful to replace.

I hope you won't need it, but it's called 2pcs L/R Left Right Shoulder Button with Flex Cable Fit for Nintend 3DS Replacement Parts on ali for about â‚¬3.50 (shipping incl.).

To reassemble it, place it over its slot and press down.

It clips in surprisingly easily.

Now we'll work on the top part. Unscrew any of the highlighted screws.

I didn't have two of these so I added new ones. Don't worry if you don't have any spares. Take a photo to remember where they were.

(Sorry for not having a good quality photo here.)

Remove the stylus (if it's in) and then remove the big plastic cover. Simply raise it. If you removed every screw it should come out easily.

Remove the old SD slot slider thing and put the new one in place.

Don't mess up the easiest step.

To reassemble it, simply follow the steps backwards.

That's it! Enjoy your brand new, fixed 3DS with a functioning SD slot!

And don't use the ifixit guide to change the pad.
